iPhone Air - A Light Review

Sometimes less isn’t more

by Brandon Butler
October 20th, 2025

The iPhone Air is fine. After a month with Apple’s thinnest iPhone ever, I don’t hate it, but I won’t be a repeat buyer. It's a product designed by a series of awkward trade-offs and when the iPhone 18 drops in 11 months, I’ll be switching back to the Pro line.

Battery life is decent—I can end the day at 20% after heavy use. Your milage may vary, especially if you’re a heavy mobile gamer or social media user (I avoid that shit). The iPhone 16 Pro required far more effort to drain. Don’t expect to hit Disneyland without a massive battery pack.

Losing the macro camera is a big deal. I used it for close-ups of leaves, cat paws, and tiny text I can’t read without glasses. Now I photograph the text with the 2x lens and zoom in—an annoying, slower workaround. Losing 5x/8x zoom isn’t a huge deal; I rarely carried zoom lenses on my SLR either. Night mode is still solid. I don’t do a lot of selfies, but I’ve played with the front-facing camera and it works.

The iPhone Air also drops Apple’s ProRAW photos and Log recording in the Camera app. This is a much more niche loss. Ditto with the LiDAR system, although the Measure app is still amazingly accurate without it. Also missing: USB-C 3.0. But considering RAW and Log aren’t here, maybe you really don’t need it?

The Air is fine—but with a series of awkward trade-offs I wouldn’t make again.

Durability is good—I’ve dropped it on hardwood several times with no damage. The screen hits a sweet spot between the Pro and Pro Max.

But the Air isn’t dramatically smaller than the Pro line. Millimeters in thickness come at the cost of battery and camera features, and I don’t think the trade-off is worth it.

Otherwise, it’s an iPhone. And Liquid Glass is terrible. 

  1. Speaking of the camera, I ran into a weird problem the other day. The Apple Camera app froze, and swiping up to quit and relaunch just gave a black screen. The Apple Intelligence camera and all other apps using the camera were black too—the hardware/software combo had failed. Even holding Power + Volume didn’t bring up the shutdown screen. I had to go into Settings, General, Shut Down. For a moment I wondered: Is my power button broken? Will the phone even turn back on? It took about a full minute of holding Power and releasing and trying again, but it finally did. The camera started working, but the Camera Plateau got extremely hot. I set the phone to Low Power Mode for twenty minutes to cool it down. Hopefully this is just a one-time iPhone Air glitch. ↩︎
