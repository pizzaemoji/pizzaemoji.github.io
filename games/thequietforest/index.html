How to Play

The Quiet Forest

Deep in the Northern California coastal redwoods, the world gets quiet. The fog rolls in off the Pacific, soft as a whisper. The trees creak gently overhead. And somewhere out there, just beyond what you can see, a little nose twitches. A tail flicks. Something is hiding — can you spot it?

The Quiet Forest is a two-player game of search and discovery.

Each player hides a set of animals somewhere in their forest. Take turns calling out grid squares, trying to find your friend's animals before they find yours. The first player to spot every animal in their friend's forest wins.

The Setup

Each player opens the app on their own phone or device. Enter your name and your friend's name, then place your animals secretly on your grid. Your friend shouldn't see your screen during setup. Animals can be placed horizontally or vertically. Tap a placed animal on the setup grid to rotate it. When all five animals are hidden, tap Start.

Taking Turns

Decide who goes first. On your turn, call out a grid square to your friend. For example, you might say, "Do I see an animal at B3?" Your friend checks their forest and responds:

Spotted — there's an animal in that square. Mark it green on your search grid and take another turn. Important: don't reveal which animal they've spotted until they've found the entire animal!
Nothing there — that square is empty. Mark it with a dot. Now it's your friend's turn.

On your friend's turn, when they call out a square check your forest grid and respond with what you see. Remember, if they spot an animal, don't reveal the name until they've found the entire animal.

Whenever a player finds all the squares of an animal, let them know — "You found the raccoon!"

Winning

When one player has found every animal in their friend's forest, they win. Be sure to congratulate them: "You found all of my animals! Great job!"

The Game Board

Swipe up and down to move between your two grids:

🔍
Top board — your friend's forest. This is where you track your searches.
🌲
Bottom board — your own forest. Tap your animal squares green when your friend spots them, so you know what they've found.
